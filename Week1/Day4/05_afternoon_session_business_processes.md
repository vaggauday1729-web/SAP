### What is a Business Process?

**Definition:** A business process is a series of steps or activities that a business performs to achieve a specific goal or produce a specific result.

### Key Characteristics:

âœ… **Has a clear goal** - What are we trying to achieve?
âœ… **Has steps** - What do we need to do?
âœ… **Has inputs** - What do we need to start?
âœ… **Has outputs** - What do we produce?
âœ… **Involves people or systems** - Who/what does the work?

## ðŸª Part 2: Real-World Business Process Examples

### Example 1: Coffee Shop - Making a Coffee

**Process Goal:** Make a perfect cup of coffee for a customer

**Process Steps:**
1. Customer orders coffee
2. Cashier takes order and payment
3. Order goes to barista
4. Barista prepares coffee
5. Coffee is served to customer
6. Customer pays (if not paid already)

**Inputs:** Coffee beans, milk, cup, customer order
**Outputs:** Coffee, satisfied customer, payment

### Example 2: Online Shopping - Ordering a Product

**Process Goal:** Customer receives ordered product

**Process Steps:**
1. Customer browses website
2. Customer adds product to cart
3. Customer checks out
4. Payment is processed
5. Order is confirmed
6. Warehouse picks product
7. Product is packed
8. Product is shipped
9. Customer receives product

**Inputs:** Customer, product, payment
**Outputs:** Delivered product, happy customer

## ðŸ’» Part 5: Enterprise Systems - The Technology Behind Processes

### What is an Enterprise System?

**Definition:** Enterprise systems are large software applications that help organizations manage their business processes and operations across the entire company.

### Key Characteristics:

âœ… **Used by many people** - Multiple employees use it
âœ… **Connects departments** - Different parts of company share information
âœ… **Manages complex processes** - Handles many business activities
âœ… **Stores lots of data** - Keeps track of everything
âœ… **Integrates everything** - Connects different business functions

---

### Components of Enterprise Systems

Think of an enterprise system like a house with different rooms:

#### 1. **Database** (The Foundation)
- **What it is:** Where all information is stored
- **Like:** A huge filing cabinet with all company data
- **Example:** Customer information, product details, financial records

#### 2. **Applications** (The Rooms)
- **What they are:** Different software programs for different tasks
- **Like:** Different rooms for different purposes (kitchen for cooking, bedroom for sleeping)
- **Example:** Finance application, HR application, Sales application

#### 3. **User Interface** (The Doors and Windows)
- **What it is:** How people interact with the system
- **Like:** The way you enter and see inside a house
- **Example:** Screens, buttons, forms that users see and use

#### 4. **Integration Layer** (The Hallways)
- **What it is:** Connects different parts of the system
- **Like:** Hallways connecting rooms in a house
- **Example:** APIs and connectors that let different applications share data
